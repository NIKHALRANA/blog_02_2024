The societal impacts of rapid technological change are evident across numerous domains, including healthcare (mRNA vaccines), industry (automation), transportation (drones), and environmental management (climate change technologies). The speed at which these technologies advance can significantly influence their societal effects. Rapid improvement in a technology's performance can serve as a strong signal of its potential importance and transformative power.

University of Minnesota researchers have created a breakthrough material for high-power electronics, potentially transforming the industry with devices that are faster, more efficient, and more transparent. Robot That Watched Surgical Videos Now Operates With Human-Level Skill

https://scitechdaily.com/robot-that-watched-surgical-videos-now-operates-with-human-level-skill/ November 13, 2024

Researchers from Johns Hopkins and Stanford Universities have advanced autonomous robotic surgery using imitation learning, enabling a robot to learn surgical techniques from a large collection of recorded procedures rather than being programmed step-by-step. This approach holds promise for reducing medical errors and improving surgical precision, marking a significant step forward in autonomous robotic surgeries.
